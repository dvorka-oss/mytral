About My Training Log

"Consistent hard work, day after day, week after week, year after year. No magic bullets, no shortcuts."

Josh Cox

My Training Log (MyTraL) is personal sport application which combines digitized data from various sources like paper training logs, XLS and Google Sheets training logs, strava.com, concept2.com and other services I used over the time.

Apart to data aggregation (workouts, activities, exercises, gear, routes, and experiences), the application defines data normalization so that the data can be analyzed, visualized and used for the creation of personalized machine learning models to predict injuries, sicknesses, and prepare training plans. MyTraL can be seen as editor for the training data in a normalized form.

Important part of the application is personal diary where I can write down my thoughts, feelings, and experiences. It can be a great way to reflect on my day, set goals, and track my progress. Keeping a diary can help me stay organized, reduce stress, and improve my mental health. It can also be a fun way to look back on my life and see how much I've grown and changed over time.

Atop of what sport trackers - like belowed strava.com provides - MyTraL adds metadata of various types - knowledge and wisdom learned over the years (diary), injuries and sicknesses tracking, gear tracking (from cost to maintenance intervals), routes (informally described, personal bests), outfits (what to wear and when), etc. so that all the sport related knowledge is in one place.

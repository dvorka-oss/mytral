About 3D impulse-response model

3D Impulse-Response Model is a novel power-based approach to modeling training load and performance in endurance sports. In contrast to traditional models that rely on a single metric, it models training load in 3 independent dimensions:

  • Explosive Power (Pmax)
    • Maximal instantaneous power capability (PCr dimension).
  • Aero Generator (CP)
    • Highest sustainable oxidative power (aerobic dimension).
  • Prime Battery (W′)
    • Finite work capacity above CP (glycolytic dimension).

Strain score (SS) is computed from second-by-second power and current Maximum Power Available (MPA), then split into:

  • SS CP
    • Strain from time spent above CP, i.e. the aerobic generator dimension.
  • SS W′
    • Strain from work done above CP, i.e. the prime battery dimension.
  • SS Pmax
    • Strain from time spent above Pmax, i.e. the explosive power dimension.

Resources:

  • Kontro, H., Mastracci, A., Cheung, S. S., & MacInnis, M. J. (2025). The three-dimensional impulse-response model: Modeling the training process in accordance with energy system-specific adaptation. arXiv preprint arXiv:2503.14841. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2503.14841

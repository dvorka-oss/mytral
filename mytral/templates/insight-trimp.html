About TRIMP

TRIMP (Training Impulse) is a sophisticated training load analytics model used to quantify the physiological stress of a workout. It is fundamentally heart-rate based, calculating cumulative effort by factoring in your heart rate reserve, duration, and the exponential intensity of the session. Its biggest strength is its ability to objectively balance long-term fitness gains against short-term fatigue, giving athletes a precise blueprint for peak performance and injury prevention.

In addition to daily TRIMP scores, the model and its chart also tracks three key metrics:

  • ATRIMP (Acute TRIMP)
    • A 7-day exponentially weighted moving average of your daily TRIMP scores, representing short-term training load and fatigue.
  • CTRIMP (Chronic TRIMP)
    • A 42-day exponentially weighted moving average of your daily TRIMP scores, representing long-term training load and fitness.
  • TRIMPB (TRIMP Balance)
    • The clean delta between your fitness and your fatigue, calculated as CTRIMP - ATRIMP, showing the balance between short-term load and long-term load, i.e. the delta between fitness and fatigue.

Chart legend:

  • TRIMP load is represented by colored dots for each day, with color indicating the level of daily strain:
    •   ~ < 50 recovery/easy
    •   ~ 50 - 120 moderate
    •   ~ > 120 hard
  • blue line ~ ATRIMP
  • green line ~ CTRIMP
  • orange line ~ TRIMPB

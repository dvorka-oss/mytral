What these parameters do

The Banister model predicts your performance as:

p(t) = k1 · fitness(t) − k2 · fatigue(t)

Fitness and fatigue are two exponential moving averages of your daily TRIMP, each with its own time constant (τ). The model is individual — some athletes recover quickly (fitness-dominated), others carry fatigue longer (fatigue-dominated). Tune these parameters so your performance line reflects how you actually feel.

Key insight — the k1/k2 ratio

With k1=1.0 and k2=2.0 (the defaults), fatigue counts 2× more than fitness. For performance to be positive, your fitness must be twice your fatigue — a high bar that means most days will read negative. This matches the Morton et al. 1990 paper's illustrative example, but your ratio may differ.

If you see this… Try this…
Performance always negative, even when you feel good Lower k2 toward 1.0 or raise k1
Performance barely moves after hard blocks Lower τ2 (fatigue responds faster)
Recovery days don't seem to help Raise γ (recovery credit) or lower the recovery threshold
Performance peaks too late after a taper Lower τ2 so fatigue dissipates faster
